### TECHNIQUE 1: Role Playing (Ideas #1–42)

#### Persona 1: Jessa — Customer on Front Website (24, Graphic Designer, QC)

**[Front Website #1]: Branch-First Product Discovery**
_Concept:_ The website is NOT a traditional e-commerce store — it's a real-time product availability checker + branch locator with optional purchase. Customer searches a product, sees which branches have it, decides: pick up or deliver.
_Novelty:_ Most retail sites push you to buy online. RedBox pushes you to the nearest physical store first — the website serves the stores, not the other way around.

**[Front Website #2]: Pick-Up or Deliver — Customer's Call**
_Concept:_ After finding a product, customer chooses: "Reserve & Pick Up at [Branch Name]" or "Deliver to Me." Pick-up is free and instant. Delivery has a fee and timeline. The choice is front and center, not buried.
_Novelty:_ The pick-up path drives foot traffic — once Jessa's in the store, she might buy MORE. The website becomes a foot traffic engine disguised as a product catalog.

**[Front Website #3]: Real-Time Stock as the Core Feature**
_Concept:_ The website's primary value proposition is real-time accuracy. "If the website says it's there, it's THERE." Powered by Convex real-time subscriptions — every POS sale, every stock movement instantly updates what customers see.
_Novelty:_ This trust factor becomes the brand promise. Customers learn they can rely on it — unlike competitors where "in stock" often means "maybe."

**[Front Website #4]: Pre-Visit Stock Check — "Don't Waste My Trip"**
_Concept:_ The #1 customer frustration is traveling to a branch only to find the item isn't there. The website solves this completely — check before you go, see real-time stock, know for certain.
_Novelty:_ Positions RedBox as respectful of the customer's time — a huge differentiator in the PH market where commuting to a mall is a real investment of time and money.

**[Front Website #5]: In-Store Mobile Scanner — "Your Phone is Your Shopping Assistant"**
_Concept:_ Customer scans any product's barcode while in-store to see sizes, colors, availability at this branch and nearby ones, plus style suggestions. No app install — just the mobile website with the html5-qrcode scanner.
_Novelty:_ Turns every customer's phone into a personal shopping assistant without needing staff. Staff are freed up for selling, not stock-checking.

#### Persona 2: Kuya Renz — Branch Manager (35, Glorietta Branch)

**[Branch Manager #6]: Morning Command Center — "Your Day in 30 Seconds"**
_Concept:_ Renz opens the dashboard and sees one screen: today's sales target vs. yesterday, 5 products about to run out, incoming transfers, staff on shift, and an AI summary: "Your Medium windbreakers will sell out by Saturday at current pace. Reorder now?"

**[Branch Manager #7]: Smart Size Curve Alerts**
_Concept:_ AI tracks which sizes sell fastest at EACH branch. Alerts: "You always run out of Medium before Large at this branch. Your next reorder should be 60% Medium, 25% Large, 15% Small."
_Novelty:_ Every branch has different size demographics — mall near university skews smaller, business district skews medium/large. AI learns each branch's pattern.

**[Branch Manager #8]: "Ghost Stock" Detector**
_Concept:_ System flags when a product shows stock but hasn't sold in 14+ days. Alert: "You have 12 units of Red Hoodie Large that haven't moved. Possible: misplaced in stockroom, display issue, or dead stock."
_Novelty:_ Catches the invisible problem — items that exist in the system but aren't actually findable on the floor.

**[Branch Manager #9]: One-Tap Branch-to-Branch Transfer**
_Concept:_ Renz sees SM North needs Small windbreakers. He taps "Transfer 10 units" — system creates the movement record, notifies SM North, deducts from inventory, driver picks it up on next route. No Viber. No phone calls.
_Novelty:_ Stock transfers become as easy as sending a message, with full audit trail and automatic inventory adjustment on both ends.

**[Branch Manager #10]: Wednesday Warning — "Weekend Rush Predictor"**
_Concept:_ Every Wednesday, AI analyzes: last 4 weekends' sales velocity, current stock levels, incoming deliveries, and payday schedule. Generates: "These 8 items will run out before Sunday. Recommended action: emergency reorder or transfer."
_Novelty:_ Gives Renz a 3-day head start. Considers Philippine payday cycles (15th and 30th) which spike sales.

**[Branch Manager #11]: Shrinkage Investigator**
_Concept:_ When physical count doesn't match system count, the audit log traces every movement: received, sold via POS, transferred, returned. Shows exact discrepancy timeline and involved staff.
_Novelty:_ Turns a mystery into a detective trail. Not accusatory — just transparent data for root cause analysis.

**[Branch Manager #12]: Holiday & Event Impact Forecaster**
_Concept:_ AI maintains a Philippine calendar engine — Valentine's Day, graduation season (March-May), back-to-school (June), Undas, 11.11/12.12 sale culture, fiestas by region, long weekends, UAAP season. Warns weeks in advance with historical sales data.
_Novelty:_ Goes beyond generic holidays — understands PH-specific events and regional patterns. Also flags "dead periods" for reduced ordering.

**[Branch Manager #13]: Typhoon & Weather-Driven Stocking**

_Novelty:_ Weather-reactive inventory — nobody in PH apparel does this.

**[Branch Manager #14]: "Dead Day" Labor Optimizer**
_Concept:_ AI identifies historically slow days and suggests reduced staffing with productive alternative activities: stockroom audit, display refresh, receiving transfers.
_Novelty:_ Turns dead periods into productive periods instead of wasted payroll.

**[POS Cashier #18]: Lightning Barcode Scan**
